During the 1980s, early graphics processors were created to handle simple 2D graphics tasks, primarily in arcade systems and computers.


Atari ANTIC microprocessor on an Atari 130XE motherboard

Arcade system boards have used specialized graphics circuits since the 1970s. In early video game hardware, RAM for frame buffers was expensive, so video chips composited data together as the display was being scanned out on the monitor.


80387 Co-processor with 80386. 80387 add to the CPU to greatly enhance mathematical calculation speeds.

It functions as a co-processor, designed to offload redundant and computationally intensive tasks that require a high degree of concurrency to achieve optimal frame rates in modern 3D graphics.
